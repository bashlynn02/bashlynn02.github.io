Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

Using QGIS, I opened the downloaded Natural Earth data and used the feature in the bottom right of QGIS to view the data in different projections. I opened these in Print Layout and exported the labeled projection maps as PNGs.

WGS84 Projection

This projection doesn't show any obvious distortion.

Aitoff Projection

This projection shows some distortion due to its angle, increasing the further it gets from the center of the projection.

Now, you should add the following projections on your own:

EPSG: 3857, 53018, 54034, 54027, 102016, and two additional projections that you choose.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

This projection shows distortion toward the bottom of the projection.

Sphere Winkel I Projection

This projection is distorted similar to the Aitoff Projection; its angle creates great distortion on the left and right sides.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

This projection isn't distorted by angle but is distorted to have a smaller longitude than latitude.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

This projection is distorted as the northern area is smaller than the southern area.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

This is a similar projection to the last but is further distorted by the enlarged blank space.

INRF 2014 Projection

This is a unique projection compared to the other projections as you can't view a majority of the Earth.

Sphere Eckert VI Projection

This is a similar projection to the others distorted by angle.
